A second pass read the entire generate-course15.mjs and generate-course15-figures.mjs from top to bottom. Three additional issues were found and fixed:

- **Fix 1 — Section 2.3, plate agglutination unsourced comparison:** The sentence "It's quick and inexpensive, which is why it's still used for routine screening, although it's less precise for tracking titer trends over time than ELISA or AGID" had no citation, and source [1] (Bowes) only lists plate agglutination as a method name without describing its comparative properties. - **Fix 2 — Figure 2.1 body caption, incorrect test attribution:** The caption read "In a positive AGID or plate agglutination test, the test-well line joins (fuses with) the control line." Precipitin lines in gel are specific to AGID (agar gel immunodiffusion). Plate agglutination produces visible clumps on a flat surface, not precipitin lines. The caption was corrected to: "In a positive AGID result, the test-well precipitin line joins (fuses with) the positive control line in the gel."
- **Fix 3 — Figure 15.3 SVG title, incorrect test attribution:** The figure title read "Reading a Precipitin-Line Test (AGID / Plate Agglutination)." The precipitin-line diagram only illustrates AGID; including "Plate Agglutination" in the title misrepresents how that test works. Title corrected to "Reading an AGID Precipitin-Line Test." Figure regenerated.

Source [7] (BioChek) uses: <40% Excellent, 40-60% Good, >60% Need to Improve. The course presents BioChek's ranges as "current guidance" cited to [7], which is correct and clear.
